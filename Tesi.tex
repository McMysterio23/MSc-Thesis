\title{{ \texttt{Picosecond Jitter of Picosecond Pulses}}}
\author{Andrea Maccarinelli}
\studentid{4535286}                           % check you student ID, LaTeX does not do this
\abstract{Quantum computing and quantum communication rely on precise manipulation of optical pulses, making the study of their timing properties critical. This thesis focuses on analyzing the timing jitter of picosecond optical pulses used for QD excitation, while femtosecond pulses were only employed as a low-jitter reference to assess the detector's response and validate the measurement setup. Two main experimental studies are presented: first, measurements on electro-optically generated pulses, and second, optimization of TCSPC and HBT measurements for a femtosecond pulsed laser. Both studies were carried out using superconducting-nanowire single photon detectors (SNSPDs). The study introduces a method to identify the central peak in HBT correlations from pulsed laser light and highlights the importance of properly setting electronic thresholds in the detection system to reduce undesired noise and double counts. These results provide an already structured starting point to assess the temporal jitter of the optical pulses used for QD excitation, ultimately contributing to improved single-photon indistinguishability.}     % limit your self to 1/2 page or 500 words
