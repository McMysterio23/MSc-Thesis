\studentid{4535286}                           % check you student ID, LaTeX does not do this
\abstract{Quantum computing and quantum communication rely on precise manipulation of optical pulses, making the study of their timing properties critical. This thesis focuses on analyzing the timing jitter of picosecond optical pulses, using femtosecond pulses as a low-jitter reference to validate the measurement setup and detector response. The study introduces a novel method to identify the central peak in intensity correlations from pulsed laser light. Additionally, it highlights the importance of properly setting electronic thresholds in the detection system to reduce undesired noise and erroneous multiple detections. Two main experimental studies are presented: first, measurements on electro-optically generated pulses, and second, optimization of time-correlated measurements for a femtosecond pulsed laser. These results constitute a starting point to assess in detail the temporal jitter of the optical pulses widely used in modern quantum optics.}     % limit your self to 1/2 page or 500 words
